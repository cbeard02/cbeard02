Clear Sign Redesign: Reimagining a Mortgage Closing Platform from a Mobile-First Perspective

MobileFirst approach
ParallelDesign exploration
7Usability test participants

01 · Overview

About Clear Sign

Clear Sign is a digital mortgage closing platform used by Rocket Mortgage, built specifically for the closing experience with both in-person and remote closing options. The goal of this project was to re-imagine what Clear Sign could become from a mobile-first approach, specifically to improve the remote closing experience. I worked with another designer in parallel to maximize the range of possible solutions explored.

My contributions focused on a parallel design option covering new ideas for the Signer and Notary paths within the product, starting from NPS survey feedback, Google Analytics session data, a thorough competitive analysis, and a usability baseline established through live usability studies.

02 · Determining the Problem

Usability Study & Data Analysis

Before designing anything, I established a clear baseline of where the current product was failing. I defined research goals, noted assumptions about the experience, and ran an unmoderated test via Usertesting.com with 7 participants matching our expected user criteria.

Results showed testers rated the check-in process as easy, yet felt uncertain about what to do once they completed it. Key recommendations surfaced:

Audio/Video expectations

Explore updating and clarifying expectations on the Audio/Video setup page. Users were unsure if they'd completed it correctly.

Redundant steps

Reduce redundant pages and information that don't help the process and made the experience feel longer than necessary.

Lobby page clarity

Create better expectations when returning to the lobby page. Next steps should be clear, and check-in completion should be confirmed.

Beyond the usability study, I sifted through NPS surveys, support tickets, and Google Analytics, tracking clicks, time on page, drop-off points, and downloads. Patterns that emerged included: where failure points clustered, user concern around what happened to their information, and unclear next steps throughout the flow.

03 · New Ideas

Restructuring the Closing Experience

Clear Sign has two major events: a pre-signing portal (view and interact with documents before closing) and the closing room (video conference check-in with a notary). My approach: move as much interaction as possible out of the closing room and into the pre-signing portal to reduce friction at the most stressful moment.

The core questions driving every design decision:

What can we automate?

Reduce the number of required user interactions by automating steps that don't need human decision-making.

Can we move processes earlier?

Move tasks out of the closing room into the pre-signing portal where lower stakes allow for more exploration and error recovery.

What information is truly needed?

Content must be useful and precise, with no filler. Users always need to know what to do and what comes next.

Access to help at all times

Users expressed concern about getting stuck. Help access and status clarity needed to be available throughout every state.

Updating the Closing Room

The notary is the facilitator. Designs evolved from displaying basic information to gradually surfacing user status and check-in progress, giving the notary confidence about where each participant stood without interrupting the process.

Updating the Pre-Signing Portal

The goal here was to provide sufficient instruction on what the user needs to accomplish, while giving them contextual information to build confidence before they enter the closing room.

04 · Reflection

What Happened Next

This project was unfinished. I was laid off at this stage of the design process. The parallel designs represented promising directions grounded in solid research, competitive analysis, and usability data. The intent was that the team would evaluate these with usability testers and iterate from there.

Methods Used

  • NPS survey analysis: Categorized and linked feedback from multiple systems to surface patterns
  • Unmoderated usability testing: 7 participants via Usertesting.com against a pre-defined criteria
  • Google Analytics: Clicks, time on page, drop-off points, and downloads
  • Competitive analysis: Mapped expectations from similar technology and processes
  • Parallel design: Two designers working independently to maximize solution range
  • Design system implementation: Applied newly adopted design system throughout all new work
