01 � Overview

About Downshift

Downshift was a social media startup built around automotive meetups. Its goal was to create an easy way for attendees and car owners to register for events, while giving the community the ability to like, follow, and view their favorite vehicles. I was brought in to create the initial MVP design for the platform, collaborating closely with founders to identify user roles, workflows, and the key features needed to serve three distinct user groups.

02 � Defining Our Users

Three Distinct User Groups

One of the founders conducted the majority of the research, interviewing attendees, vehicle owners, and event organizers to gather insights into their experiences, needs, and pain points.

Attendees

Had to apply through various disconnected sites to join events. No centralized platform for signing up for multiple meet-ups. No way to know if an event was full until arrival.

Vehicle Owners

Had to coordinate with organizers via email, phone, or in person to confirm space availability, complete sign-ups, and sign legal waivers. This was often time-consuming with tight deadlines.

Event Coordinators

Managed communications manually via emails, texts, and calls. Challenged to balance attendance, ensuring enough show up without overcrowding, while handling verification manually.

04 � Key Features

Event Search & Virtual Garage

Event Search & Details

Streamlined participation for both attendees and vehicle owners through event discovery by location or interest, comprehensive event pages, ticket purchases, and seamless vehicle owner sign-ups including spot reservations and documentation.

Virtual Garage

A dedicated space for vehicle owners to showcase their cars through detailed profiles with photos, specs, and unique features; quick RSVP to events; and community engagement through likes and follows.

05 � Outcome & Reflection

What We Learned

The product was soft launched with just the virtual garage features, focusing on the social media aspect of the app. It wasn't received well. It didn't offer enough value over the social media platforms users were already using to show off their vehicles. Given funding challenges and other factors, it was decided not to continue the project.

Key Takeaway

  • Understanding market need: When developing a product that addresses a problem you're passionate about, it's essential to conduct thorough market research to identify which features should be prioritized for the initial release. The core value proposition (event management) needed to launch alongside the social features, not after.
